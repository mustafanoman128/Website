About me

Some people see spreadsheets and quietly look for the exit. I tend to lean in instead. Patterns inside messy datasets, strange revenue fluctuations, campaign performance that doesn't quite add up — those are the kinds of puzzles that pull my attention.

Based in Chicago, I work as a data analyst focused on translating large, often chaotic data environments into insights that business leaders can actually use — without needing a statistics textbook on their desk.

Academically, my path ran through the University of Illinois at Chicago, where I completed a Master's degree in Analytics. Economics formed the earlier backbone of my education, which probably explains why I tend to think about numbers in terms of incentives, behavior, and real economic outcomes rather than just charts.

What colleagues tend to mention first is something simpler: I ask good questions. Sometimes too many, maybe, but usually the useful kind.
